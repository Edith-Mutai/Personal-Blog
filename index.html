Born A Crime - Trevor Noah

"Love does not build cages."

Dive into Reflections

In Born a Crime, Trevor Noah writes, “Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'” This quote sharply reveals a contradiction still seen in modern relationships: the desire for freedom-loving women — but also the urge to control them. Today’s society loudly advocates for women’s independence, ambition, and self-expression. Yet, many relationship expectations haven’t evolved at the same pace. Some men still find strong, opinionated women attractive — but struggle when those women refuse to be controlled or silenced. The idea of wanting someone because of their freedom, only to later suppress it, is a painful irony still alive. We see examples everywhere. In workplaces, women who lead confidently are often labeled "bossy" or "difficult." In personal relationships, ambitious women are sometimes criticized for not being “nurturing enough.” Even popular culture often romanticizes the "taming" of the "wild woman" trope. Personally, I’ve noticed how society sometimes applauds free-spirited women — as long as their freedom doesn’t threaten traditional power dynamics. True love, however, should never involve cages. It should be a partnership where both people grow freely, not a constant struggle for dominance. Trevor Noah’s mother, Patricia, embodies this fierce independence. Her refusal to be caged is what kept her spirit alive — even in the most dangerous circumstances. Her story reminds us that real love celebrates strength, not stifles it. In today’s world, we must question: Do we truly love people as they are — or are we trying to turn them into something safer for ourselves?

Recommendations

Becoming

"There is no limit to what we, as women, can accomplish." - Michelle Obama

Becoming is a memoir by Michelle Obama, published in 2018. The book chronicles her life from childhood to her role as the First Lady of the United States, offering insights into her personal and professional journey.

The Alchemist

"We are travelers on a cosmic journey, stardust, swirling and dancing in the eddies and whirlpools of infinity. Life is eternal. We have stopped for a moment to encounter each other, to meet, to love, to share. This is a precious moment. It is a little parenthesis in eternity.” - Paul Coelho

The Alchemist, is a novel by Brazilian author Paulo Coelho which was first published in 1988. Originally written in Portuguese, it became a widely translated international bestseller.

Home Going

"We are all of us made of stories. We are all of us made of stories. We are all of us made of stories." - Yaa Gyasi

Homegoing is a novel by Ghanaian-American author Yaa Gyasi, published in 2016. The book follows the lives of two half-sisters, Effia and Esi, and their descendants over the course of 300 years, exploring the impact of family, history, and the legacy they leave behind.

How To Raise A Feminist

"Feminism is not a dirty word. It is a word that means equality. It is a word that means freedom. It is a word that means love." - Caitlin Moran

How to Raise a Feminist is a book by Caitlin Moran, published in 2018. The book offers practical advice and insights on how to raise children with feminist values, encouraging parents to foster a sense of equality and empowerment in their children.

Who Moved My Cheese

"The quicker you let go of old cheese, the sooner you find new cheese." - Spencer Johnson

Who Moved My Cheese? is a motivational business fable by Spencer Johnson, published in 1998. The book uses a simple parable to illustrate the importance of adapting to change and embracing new opportunities in both personal and professional life.
